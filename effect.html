01 Superconductivity Basics

Meissner

The Meissner Effect is a fascinating phenomenon observed in superconductors, where they expel virtually all magnetic flux from their interior when cooled below a critical temperature. This expulsion of magnetic fields results in the superconductor becoming perfectly diamagnetic, meaning it actively repels magnetic fields.

Magnetic susceptibility is a fundamental property of materials that quantifies their response to an applied magnetic field. In the context of superconductors, magnetic susceptibility plays a crucial role in understanding their behavior.

Vortex

In the realm of superconductivity, vortex matter refers to a fascinating and intricate pattern that emerges when a superconductor is subjected to a magnetic field. When magnetic flux penetrates a superconducting material, it forms tiny whirlpools of electrical current called vortices.

Quantum
