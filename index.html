room-temperature ambient-pressure conductor

Room-temperature superconductivity has long been the holiest of holy grails in condensed-matter physics. Within the past decade, the appearance of new materials that superconduct at relatively balmy temperatures, but only under extreme pressures, has brought a slight yet significant alteration in the quest.
